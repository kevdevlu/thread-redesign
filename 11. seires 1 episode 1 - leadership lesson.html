Read Zephaniah 3:17
 Close your eyes and imagine God for a few moments. What do you see? Write down some of His attributes.

How has your image of God changed? What was it like…
As a kid?
As a college student?
5-10 years ago?

As we grow in Christ, our image of God grows and matures. 

In what ways might your image of God be skewed? What attributes of God would you like to develop? 
 Read Ephesians 5:22-33
 Often, when churches have retreats or milestone services, they take a group picture. It’s interesting to look back at those images and see how the church has grown or changed. In Ephesians 5, Paul writes that the church is an image that reflects God. In his teaching on marriage, he reveals that the church should be sacrificial and serving, loving and respectful, just like a good marriage. The relational unity of the church and our marriages reflects the unity of the Father, Son, and Spirit in the Trinity. Surely, just as your church has changed over the years, the image of God it has projected to the surrounding community has also changed.

People can’t see God, but they can see Christians and churches who are called to bear the image of God. Take a moment to picture your church.
