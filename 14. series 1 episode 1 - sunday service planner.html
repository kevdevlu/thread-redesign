Series 1: Starting Point Introduction

Introduction
Sunday Service Planner

Welcome Scripture:  This will be one verse, or part of a verse, to focus the welcome message and ready people’s hearts for worship. The verse can be a part of the welcome or it can be used as a jumping off point for a welcoming prayer. Or both.
 Example: Zephaniah 3:17b “The Lord your God is…a mighty one who will save.”
“Welcome to our church. I thought a lot about this passage this week and I am so grateful that God has saved me with his might. I think about this time in my life when I was really in need of help and love and God showed up…Maybe some of us here today are in need of a powerful and mighty savior. That’s exactly who our God is and he is here with us today. Let’s pray…Mighty warrior…
All from Zephaniah 3:17:Week 0 - "The Lord your God is in your midst" - You can introduce liturgy during this welcome and explain that passages will come from Zephaniah 3:17 for the next 4 weeks
Week 1 - "A mighty one who will save"
Week 2 - "He will rejoice over you with gladness"
Week 3 - "He will quiet you by his love"
Week 4 - "He will exult over you with loud singing."

 Readings:  This may be a new element for some. It couldn’t be more simple. Select someone from the congregation or from the worship team to read a passage of scripture. No commentary or thoughts - just read it. Thread may give an entire chapter but you can choose just a verse or two if that works better for your service times and flow. The reader could also take one verse or word from the passage and use it as a jumping off point for prayer. 
 Example: Psalm 103
One of your part singers reads Psalm 103:1-5 during a transition between two worship songs. A guitar player strums in the background.
